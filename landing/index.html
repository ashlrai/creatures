How It Works

From real biology to evolved intelligence

Neurevo bridges the gap between computational neuroscience and evolutionary optimization, starting from actual connectome data.

01

Start with Real Biology

Load actual connectome data from the C. elegans nervous system or Drosophila brain. Every neuron, every synapse — mapped from real organisms by the OpenWorm and FlyWire projects.

02

Evolve in 3D Worlds

Populations of organisms compete in MuJoCo physics environments. They navigate toward food, avoid obstacles, and must survive. Natural selection operates on neural architecture itself.

03

Discover What Matters

CMA-ES optimization and reinforcement learning accelerate evolution by 100x. Analyze which neural circuits persist across generations to understand what biological computation is truly essential.

The Science

Why this hasn't been done before

Traditional neuroevolution uses artificial neural networks. We start with the real thing.

What Makes This Novel

Neuroevolution has a 30-year history, but every prior system evolved artificial network topologies. NEAT, HyperNEAT, and their descendants create networks from scratch.

Neurevo is different. We begin with a real biological connectome — the complete neural wiring diagram of C. elegans, painstakingly mapped by researchers. Evolution operates on actual neural architecture, mutating synaptic weights, adding or removing real synapses, and even growing new neurons.

The Future of Virtual Organisms

"The FDA Modernization Act 2.0 removed mandatory animal testing requirements — virtual organisms and in-silico models are the future of biological research."

By evolving virtual organisms with biologically-accurate neural systems, we can study neural circuit function, test hypotheses about brain evolution, and accelerate neuroscience without animal models.

Key references: OpenWorm project (2014), FlyWire connectome (Dorkenwald et al., 2024), C. elegans connectome (White et al., 1986).
