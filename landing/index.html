How It Works

From DNA to behavior
in three steps

Neurevo models the full pipeline — from genetic encoding to spiking neural dynamics to embodied locomotion.

01

Encode the Genome

Each organism's neural architecture is encoded as a genome — connection weights, neurotransmitter types, ion channel densities. Mutations operate on real biological parameters.

02

Simulate the Brain

Brian2 runs a biophysically accurate spiking neural network. Real Hodgkin-Huxley dynamics, real neurotransmitter kinetics. Every spike is earned.

03

Evolve for Fitness

Organisms live in MuJoCo physics. Touch stimuli fire sensory neurons; motor output drives muscles. The fittest survive. Repeat for 100+ generations.

Built on Real Science

Standing on the shoulders
of neuroscience

Every neuron, every synapse, every connection weight comes from peer-reviewed connectomics data.

Connectome Data

OpenWorm / WormAtlas

Complete C. elegans connectome: 302 neurons (299 non-pharyngeal), 6,393 chemical synapses, 890 gap junctions. White et al. (1986), updated by Varshney et al. (2011).

Neural Simulation

Brian2 Spiking Framework

Hodgkin-Huxley neuron models with biologically realistic membrane dynamics, synaptic transmission, and neurotransmitter kinetics. Stimberg et al. (2019), eLife.

Physics Engine

MuJoCo by DeepMind

Multi-joint dynamics with contact. Sub-millisecond physics simulation for biomechanical locomotion. Todorov et al. (2012), IEEE/RSJ IROS.

Regulatory
